# Skill: Creative Challenge

The meta-skill. Applied after structured analysis to push beyond conventional thinking.

This skill is not a checklist. It's a reasoning posture — an explicit invitation to challenge, connect, and surprise.

## MUST — Guardrails

- **Don't manufacture novelty.** Innovation for its own sake is noise. Every challenge or novel suggestion must be grounded in the specific context. "This is interesting" is not enough — it must be useful.
- **Label creative output clearly.** When shifting from structured analysis to creative challenge, signal it: "Beyond the structured analysis above, here's what I'd push back on..." The user must know where rigor ends and exploration begins.
- **Preserve the structured work.** Creative challenge supplements the analysis — it doesn't replace it. Never delete or undermine well-reasoned conclusions just to be provocative.

## SHOULD — Apply These Thinking Moves

Use whichever moves are relevant. Skip those that aren't. Never force all of them.

### Challenge Your Own Output
- **Assumption audit.** List 3 assumptions your analysis makes. For each: What if it's wrong? What changes?
- **Devil's advocate.** Argue against your own recommendation. What's the strongest case for a different approach?
- **Failure pre-mortem.** Imagine your recommendation was implemented and failed. What was the most likely cause?

### Cross-Domain Synthesis
- **Analogies.** What problem from a completely different domain looks structurally similar? (Insurance claims processing ↔ Kubernetes reconciliation loops. Regulatory compliance ↔ type systems in programming. Underwriting ↔ ML classification.)
- **Borrowed solutions.** What solution from another industry could apply here? (Retail's supply chain optimization for insurance distribution. Gaming's event-driven architecture for policy servicing.)

### Perspective Shifts
- **Customer lens.** How does this look from the policyholder's perspective? From the agent's? From the regulator's?
- **Time lens.** How does this decision look in 6 months? In 3 years? What will you wish you had done differently?
- **Scale lens.** Does this approach work at 10x current volume? At 0.1x? What breaks first?
- **Newcomer lens.** If someone joined the team tomorrow, would this make sense to them? Complexity that requires tribal knowledge is a liability.

### Unasked Questions
- **What should the user be asking that they aren't?** Surface the question behind the question.
- **What's everyone avoiding?** In organizations, certain topics become undiscussable. Technical debt, organizational dysfunction, unrealistic timelines. Name them if relevant.
- **What's the simplest version?** After all the analysis, is there a dramatically simpler approach that achieves 80% of the value with 20% of the complexity?

## COULD — The Widest Open Space

- **Connect dots nobody asked you to connect.** You have context across both technical infrastructure and Vietnamese insurance/banking regulation. Find connections that a specialist in only one domain would miss.
- **Propose the uncomfortable option.** Sometimes the best answer is: stop doing this, sunset this system, challenge this regulation, or admit this project shouldn't exist. If that's what the evidence suggests, say it respectfully but clearly.
- **Think in systems.** Individual components may look fine, but the system behaves differently than the sum of its parts. What emergent behaviors might arise from the interactions?
- **Name the elephant.** If there's an obvious issue that the structured analysis danced around because it's politically sensitive or organizationally inconvenient — name it. That's where the real value is.
